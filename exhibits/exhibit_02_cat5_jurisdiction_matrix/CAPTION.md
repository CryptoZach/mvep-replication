# Exhibit 2: Category 5 jurisdiction matrix

**Jurisdiction-differentiated Category 5 (Insolvency Posture) scoring matrix: twelve product-sample regimes (J1 to J10 plus the J2S and J3F bank-variant sub-rows) and eight international comparators (J11 EU MiCA, J12 UK, J13 Hong Kong, J14 UAE-federal, J14A UAE Abu Dhabi free zone, J14Z UAE Dubai free zone, J15 Switzerland, J16 Japan), by seven dimensions (D1 to D7).**

Orientation: the instrument-anchored regimes are the X axis (each column labeled by its regime id and anchor product; the full regime names are in the regime list below) and the seven scoring dimensions are the Y axis, consistent with Exhibits 1 and 3, which carry products on the X axis.

The matrix replaces the retired undifferentiated-domicile scoring basis for Category 5. Each cell carries pass/partial/fail plus an evidence basis citing sources verified against primary texts (statutes, regulations, judgments, regulator registers); the full per-cell evidence text is in `cat5_jurisdiction_matrix_data.csv`. The matrix is the sole permissible scoring basis for Category 5 verdicts in the A2 package: every product verdict cites cells in `JnxDn` form. The matrix is necessary, not sufficient; the product-level verdict always combines the jurisdiction row with the product's own evidence (treatment memo, scenario analysis per Table 1).

## Dimensions

D1 statutory customer-asset segregation in insolvency; D2 investor claim priority regime; D3 recognition and coordination of cross-border proceedings; D4 product-specific insolvency protections (statutory vs contractual); D5 rehypothecation constraints; D6 forced-conversion risk (insolvency-law dimension only; settlement-asset defects are handled by the paper's recursive Cash Settlement Asset analysis); D7 track record of investor recovery.

## Verdict levels and markers

Pass / Partial / Fail per the rubric. **Fail*** = fail by structural inapplicability: no law could apply (J2 D1/D5: a deposit is a bank liability, there is no customer asset to segregate; J10 all cells: no legal entity exists). Rendered distinctly so an inapplicability row reads as no regime rather than as a weak regime. **Partial(+)** on J4xD5 = sub-posture split: the secured-note sub-posture earns partial (UCC 9-322 first-to-file backstop: later re-pledging cannot prime the perfected first-priority lien) while the LP-equity sub-posture fails (6 Del. C. 9-207(c)(3) affirmatively permits repledge; constraints contractual only); product derivations use the applicable sub-posture.

## Scoring rules

1. **Aggregation rule (Q1a).** Product PASS requires D1, D2, D5 all pass and no dimension fail; a fail among D3/D4/D7 caps the product at PARTIAL; partials in D1/D2/D5 with no fails yield PARTIAL; a fail among D1/D2/D5/D6 caps at AMBIGUOUS or FAIL depending on whether the product's own documentation cures the gap contractually.
2. **Structural inapplicability (Q2a).** Cells score Fail* with the marker; the product-level derivation may credit documented claim-side substitutes where the regime substitutes claim protection for asset protection (the J2 deposit regime: insurance, depositor preference, resolution practice).
3. **Enacted-but-not-in-force statutes (Q3a).** Rows score the regime operative as of the as-of date; dormant statutes (GENIUS Act; BVI Insolvency Act Part XVIII; MAS SCS framework) score partial where they would otherwise lift a cell, with standing re-score triggers: GENIUS final rules (J3 D1/D2/D4 and the USDC/PYUSD verdicts, plus the USD1 comparator verdict per ruling 11; J3 D5 passes independently on New York regulation per ruling 9a, with GENIUS adding the statutory layer at effectiveness) and Singapore SCS legislation (J9 D1/D2/D4 and the USDG verdict).
4. **D2 documentation conjunct (Q4a).** The rubric's "documented in product materials" prong is assessed at product level; the regime cell records whether statute or settled case law fixes the rank.
5. **D5 severity ladder (Q5, substance-based).** A statutory quantitative constraint or prohibition yields pass even where the express ban wording sits at code-of-practice level above a statutory floor (J7) or arises from a custody-mandate-plus-leverage-cap lattice (J1); a customer-level written-consent exception preserves pass (J9); a fund-level consent carve-out with disclosure demotes to partial (J6).
6. **USDY D5 (Q6a).** The UCC 9-322 priority backstop lifts the secured-note sub-posture to partial; the act-of-repledge constraint is contractual, and a 2026-06-14 verification of the public Ondo Global Markets documents found no express no-repledge or no-rehypothecation covenant (only the functional control-agreement lockup operated through the security agent), with the Issuer's Sales Terms (KYC-gated) the sole remaining route to the explicit covenant.
7. **El Salvador (Q7 synthesis).** D1/D2/D3 all fail. D1 is evidence-confirmed (the official CNAD regulations were read in full: no segregation, insolvency, priority, or rehypothecation provisions). D2's basis documents that a verified customer's redemption claim is determinable as a general unsecured claim after multi-jurisdiction analysis; the cell fails on the no-claim-for-most-holders branch. D3 does not credit the unverified exequatur route; an upgrade-to-partial trigger is registered if the Codigo Procesal Civil y Mercantil recognition provisions verify at acquisition.
8. **J1 residual calls (Q8a).** The statutorily flat capital structure satisfies D2 (rank determinate by statute); the Reserve Primary recovery (more than 98 cents within roughly 16 months, court-ordered) satisfies D7 timeliness.
9a. **J3xD5 re-grade.** Initially partial (operative constraint read as guidance-only). The Circle 424(b)(4) prospectus records the issuer's characterization that the MTL and BitLicense/Virtual Currency License regulations prohibit rehypothecation of reserve assets; primary-text verification of 23 NYCRR 200.9 confirmed a binding regulatory prohibition (200.9(c), customer-direction exception) plus a same-type-and-amount coverage requirement (200.9(b)), structurally identical to the Singapore reg 18C(1)(c) basis already graded pass under the ladder. Re-graded to pass for cross-row consistency; the issuer-reserve reach caveat is documented in the cell and graded consistently with J9xD5. No product verdict changes (USDC/PYUSD stay PARTIAL via J3xD1/D2/D4).
10. **USYC controlling layer (Q9, decided on the fact base).** Both layers score PARTIAL on the matrix, but the dispositive fact (shareholder of record vs beneficial owner behind a CIBL nominee position) is not established by the verified documents; the product verdict stays AMBIGUOUS on the differentiated J6 x J7 basis, upgrading if the offering memorandum (wishlisted; Reg S gated) resolves the split.
11. **USD1 trust-bank derivation (comparator).** The USD1 issuer of record (BitGo Bank & Trust, N.A., an OCC-chartered uninsured national trust bank; conditional approval 2025-12-12, converted from BitGo Trust Company, Inc., South Dakota) sits outside the J3 New York anchors: 23 NYCRR 200.9 and the NYDFS guidance layer are entity-scoped to NYDFS-supervised issuers and do not reach this charter stack (charter-stack inference; the NYDFS-chartered BitGo affiliate is not the issuer and holds no attested reserve custody). The derivation applies the J3 federal layer only: the dormant-GENIUS legs score as J3xD1/D2/D4 partial-grade in the issuer's own right (12 USC 5901 federal qualified issuer class; charter condition requiring GENIUS conformance); D5 carries no verified operative constraint (the OCC fiduciary-account rules, 12 CFR 9, are of unanalyzed applicability to the reserve trust) and grades as a fail-grade absence that fires the Q1a cap; the partial contractual cure (FBO trust titling) is unreconciled with the terms' no-property-interest clauses. Product verdict AMBIGUOUS. The rule-3 re-score trigger extends to USD1 (GENIUS final rules). The 12 CFR 51 uninsured-national-trust-bank resolution forum now hosts Paxos (PYUSD), BitGo (USD1), and Circle's de novo trust bank; this derivation is embodied as the J3F sub-row below.

## J3F sub-row

Row J3F scores the OCC uninsured-national-trust-bank variant of J3: the J3 federal layer only, with the NYDFS anchors entity-scoped away under the federal-layer derivation rule. Anchor products USD1 (issuer of record BitGo Bank & Trust, N.A., OCC Charter 25366; resolution forum 12 CFR Part 51) and PYUSD (issuer of record Paxos Trust Company, N.A., OCC Charter 25379; same forum; generalized onto the row when the consummated Paxos conversion entity-scoped the New York anchors away, with the D1/D2/D4/D5 cell bases naming both anchors and no new cells added). Cell provenance is split by origin:

- **D1, D2, D4 PARTIAL and D5 FAIL** embody the federal-layer derivation rule (the dormant-GENIUS legs in the issuer's own right; the D5 fail-grade absence of any verified operative rehypothecation constraint).
- **D3 FAIL, D6 PARTIAL, D7 FAIL** are scored against primary texts (11 U.S.C. 109(b)(2) and 1501(c)(1); 12 U.S.C. 191-200 and 194; 12 CFR Part 51 sections 51.1 to 51.9; the 2016 OCC final rule, 81 FR 92594, including its preamble's no-receiver-since-1933 record; the OCC conditional-approval letter nr-occ-2025-125c.pdf). Full evidence bases in `cat5_jurisdiction_matrix_data.csv`.
- The pattern the row surfaces: the federal trust-bank stack grades WEAKER than J3 on three dimensions (D3: outside both the Bankruptcy Code and Chapter 15 with no recognition framework or practice; D5: no operative rehypothecation constraint where the NYDFS layer carries the binding 23 NYCRR 200.9 prohibition; D7: zero modern in-forum precedent where J3 at least carries the completed adjacent-class chapter 11 record), and no stronger than J3 anywhere. The USD1 product verdict is unchanged (AMBIGUOUS; the D5 leg fires the Q1a cap independently of the new cells).
- An upgrade trigger registered by the D3 research: if a court were to hold an uninsured national trust bank Bankruptcy Code-eligible (the question is judicially untested; the OCC's position is awareness-based, not a holding), J3FxD3 would partially inherit J3's Chapter 15 posture and re-derive. This sits alongside the GENIUS-final-rules trigger, which re-derives J3FxD1/D2/D4.

## J2S sub-row (SoFiUSD insured-bank comparator)

Row J2S scores the FDIC-insured-national-bank, non-deposit-stablecoin variant of J2: the FDI Act receivership forum with the deposit-claim premises that score the J2 cells removed, because SoFiUSD is expressly not a deposit. Anchor product SoFiUSD (issuer of record SoFi Bank, N.A., FDIC Cert 26881, an insured full-service national bank; resolution forum FDIC receivership under the FDI Act). The row applies the J3F precedent in claim-class form: when the anchor regime's cells are scored on premises that dissolve for the product (NYDFS supervision for J3F; deposit-claim status for J2), a variant sub-row scores the residual regime rather than silently re-reading the parent row. All seven cells scored against the SOFID Terms of Use, the SOFID Risk Disclosure, the SoFi 2026-05-27 press release, and the cited statutes (12 USC 1813(l), 1821(d)(11), 1821(e); OCC Interpretive Letters 1174 and 1183):

- **J2xD1 Fail\* and J2xD5 Fail\* do not carry.** Both J2 inapplicability premises (a deposit is a bank liability with no customer asset to segregate; deploying depositor funds is the deposit-taking business model) dissolve for a dedicated non-deposit reserve, and the J2 claim-side substitutes (insurance, depositor preference) credited at product level under the structural-inapplicability rule are expressly disclaimed for SoFiUSD. The marker flips from Fail\* to plain FAIL on both.
- **J2xD2/D3/D4/D6/D7 carry their entity-level legs at PARTIAL and lose their deposit-specific legs.** Only D3 carries unchanged (Bankruptcy Code and Chapter 15 exclusion; FDI Act foreign-recognition gap cured by 2023 practice); D2 retains the 12 USC 1821(d)(11)(A) waterfall but places the holder at clause (iii) behind every deposit; D4 retains the FDI Act receivership machinery but loses insurance and depositor preference; D6 retains bounded receiver powers but adds the 1821(e) repudiation exposure; D7 retains a modern completed-receivership adjacent record but has zero in-class precedent.
- The product verdict is AMBIGUOUS (the Q1a cap resolved on the open holder-claim-class question). A FAIL reading rests on a documentation-worsens premise (the SOFID Terms negate any holder property interest, with no offsetting titling), but that premise is falsified by the published reserve evidence: two published Deloitte attestations show the reserve held 100 percent in cash at the Federal Reserve through a BitGo Trust Company, Inc. custodied segregated fiduciary account FBO holders, legally segregated from both SoFi's and BitGo's corporate assets. The documentation is therefore two-sided and unreconciled (FBO segregated custody attested, holder property interest disclaimed in the Terms), the USD1 Ambiguous pattern rather than the USDT documentation-worsens Fail pattern, so AMBIGUOUS is the supported verdict and FAIL is a structured alternative, not an open split. The J2 row itself is untouched (its cells stay scored on the JPMD deposit facts).
- A re-score trigger registered by the derivation: a deposit recharacterization (by FDIC determination, court holding, or conversion into the announced SoFi tokenized deposits) collapses J2S into J2; GENIUS Sec. 20 effectiveness adds an unlawful-issuance overlay absent a verified restructuring into a permitted-issuer class.

## Scoping notes

1. **US prime-broker custody leg (USYC).** The fund's assets are held through a US prime broker (Marex) in segregated accounts; asset-level segregation in a CUSTODIAN insolvency is governed by US broker-dealer custody law (SIPA; UCC Article 8), which no J row covers. Scoped out deliberately: the matrix scores issuer-layer and fund-layer regimes; the custodian leg is documented here rather than scored.
2. **Row-header annotations.** J3: the row now anchors USDC alone. Paxos (PYUSD) migrated off the state layer to the J3F federal-layer-only row on 2026-06-12 once its OCC conversion was established consummated (the staleness review); Circle's de novo national trust bank was conditionally approved the same OCC action but Circle remains NYDFS-supervised for USDC and continues to derive from J3 pending its own review; the row scores the operative layered regime. J4: USDY's issuer migrated to Ondo Global Markets (BVI) Limited on 2025-12-15 per Ondo's documentation; the row scores the UCC collateral package and the legacy Delaware structure with the BVI forum flagged and cross-referenced to J5. J10: DAI's successor USDS shares the no-entity character; protocol-level conversion is optional and 1:1, with exchange-side auto-conversion noted (April 2026).
3. **Terminology.** The exhibit-surface narrations of the retired Category 5 scoring basis are worded as "the prior undifferentiated-domicile basis."
